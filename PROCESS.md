# VISION — The Sales Process

This document defines the **end-to-end process** VISION enforces, and how each agent
fits into it. It is derived from the *i3 Agentic Sales Assistance* blueprint, the Q25
privacy deck, the 30/60/90 onboarding schedule, and the Gardewine POC.

---

## 1. The progression model

Success in VISION is defined as **movement from one stage to the next**:

```
Lead → Meeting → Proof of Concept → Documentation → Commitment → Closed revenue
```

The system's job is to ensure consistent forward movement and to stop deals from
stalling or leaking knowledge between stages.

## 3. Stages and the agents that own them

### Stage 1 — HR Onboarding · **V-Start**
- **Input:** employee name, role, region, assigned segment, experience level, manager, start date.
- **Output:** rep profile, permissions, training path, segment assignment.

### Stage 2 — Training & Readiness · **V-Coach**
- The rep goes through **i3 Live** and **i3host**, following the 30/60/90 schedule
  (Sales Mastery, Sales Operations, i3 Technology Center, Production Training,
  Strategic Thinking, Marketing, VAR network, AI tools, presentation skills),
  with mentorship and weekly reflection forms.
- **Output:** readiness score, product-knowledge profile, certification status.
- **Ramp targets:** industry 60–90 days · i3 target **30–45 days** · top performers **15–30 days**.

### Stage 3 — Account Intelligence · **V-Target**
- **Sources:** 6sense / SixthSense, HubSpot, Salesforce history, LinkedIn, company sites, press/shareholder releases.
- Maps the **whole buying group**, not one contact, and infers role-based pain points.
- **Messaging strategy by role:**
  - IT leader → integration & security story
  - Operations leader → efficiency & cost-reduction story
  - Executive → ROI & business-value story
- **Output:** account map, decision-maker list, role-based pain points, messaging strategy.

### Stage 4 — Guided Outreach · **V-Reach**
- Pushes prioritized leads into the rep's queue, tells them **who to contact first**,
  drafts email / LinkedIn / call openings, and **validates contact data** (fixes bad numbers/emails).
- **Output:** action queue, outreach content, contact-validation tasks.

### Stage 5 — Proof of Work · **V-Verify**
- The system does **not** rely on what the rep says happened — it verifies what actually happened:
  - **Email:** sent, timestamp, recipient, reply, follow-up timing.
  - **Phone:** number dialed, duration, whether a conversation occurred, transcript if available.
  - **Mobile:** company-phone work calls.
- **Output:** verified activity feeding the rep's engagement score (protects forecast accuracy).

### Stage 6 — Meeting & Presentation · **V-Present**
- Preps the deck, likely objections, and positioning; loops in the **manager** for the
  executive walkthrough; captures the meeting transcript.
- **Output:** presentation pack + structured transcript that becomes POC input.

### Stage 7 — POC · **V-Prove**
- Converts the transcript into a **scoped POC**: objective, success metrics, timeline,
  named supporters, blockers, and PM handoff. (Reference model: **Gardewine** — Toronto
  terminal, 45-day validation, incumbent Protex AI.)
- **Output:** POC document + PM-agent handoff (scope, timeline, cybersecurity forms, approvals).

### Stage 8 — Legal & Privacy · **V-Guard**  *(compliance gate)*
- Triggered when a designated i3 AI tool (facial recognition, LPR, behavioral, etc.)
  or a system integration (e.g. POS + video) is in scope.
- Runs the **four-part necessity & proportionality test**, applies the right bills by
  jurisdiction, and produces required **signage** and **documentation**.
- See **GUIDE.md** for the full privacy process. **No POC with sensitive tooling proceeds
  without clearing this gate.**

### Stage 9 — Commercial Close · **V-Close**
- Price position vs the incumbent, contract language, and the commitment to a phased rollout.
- Arrives with clean documentation, verified activity, and legal answers already captured.

### Stage 10 — Performance & Leadership · **V-Score**
- Rep scoring and **segmentation** (top / mid / developing), the KPI funnel, forecast,
  and **expert-intervention triggers**.

## 4. Entry points (start anywhere)

A rep can enter the process at the stage that matches their situation:

| Entry point | Starts at |
|-------------|-----------|
| Brand-new employee | Onboarding (V-Start) |
| Existing rep | Prospecting (V-Target) |
| Advanced rep / veteran | POC (V-Prove) |
| Partner / Integrator | Architecture overview, then plugged into the relevant flow |

Segments supported: **Grocery, Retail, QSR, Commercial, Schools, Integrator, Healthcare.**

---

## 5. KPIs

**System KPIs:** lead → meeting · meeting → POC · POC → close · time between stages.

**Rep KPIs:** engagement score · progression rate · learning application · pipeline contribution.

---

## 6. Expert-intervention logic

Senior expertise is pulled in **precisely**, only when:
- deal size exceeds a threshold,
- engagement drops,
- POC risk increases, or
- a high-value opportunity is identified.

---

## 7. The control principle

Everything entered into VISION is proprietary company knowledge, pushed into Salesforce
and i3-owned records — **so no deal knowledge walks away with the rep.** The system
requires clean data, enforced process, and consistent usage; without discipline it will
not perform.
